01 / The Problem

The High-Intent Customer Who Disappears

In high-ticket consumer retail — custom furniture, full room interiors — the buyer journey doesn't follow a clean funnel. A customer spends 45 minutes on the website: they configure a living room in a 3D editor, save the layout, add ₹3.8L of items to their cart. Then they stop at checkout.

This is not abandonment. This is a channel switch. For purchases above ₹2L, 63% of customers want to see materials, touch fabrics, and talk to someone before committing. They walk into the store.

The offline salesperson asks: "How can I help you today?"

The customer has to start from zero. 45 minutes of intent signal, a fully configured 3D layout, a considered cart — all invisible to the salesperson. The digital high-intent context is completely lost in the physical world.

"The customer walked in knowing exactly what they wanted. The failure was that the salesperson had no idea the customer had already done the work."

03 / The Hacky V1

Cart ID Dictation — A UX Disaster

Our MVP was straightforward: generate a 6-character alphanumeric Cart ID for sessions above ₹1.5L cart value. Email it to the customer. They could walk into a store, dictate the code to the salesperson, and retrieve their cart from the legacy POS system.

Adoption: 2.3%. The friction was predictable in hindsight:

  • Customers felt awkward dictating a code. "KRTX9A" feels transactional, not premium — wrong register for a high-AOV sale.
  • Salespeople found POS lookup tedious and error-prone. They often skipped it.
  • The email often wasn't opened before the store visit.

We validated the problem but not the solution. We needed a handoff mechanism as frictionless as muscle memory.

04 / The Real Solution

The Contextual QR Handshake

The insight: friction in identity resolution kills omnichannel conversion. We needed a contactless, zero-dictation handshake between a mobile browser and a store tablet — one that felt like magic rather than a process.

The QR Session Handshake Architecture
BROWSING
Customer configures 3D room on mobile. Session graph builds in real-time.
INTENT_DETECTED
Cart > ₹1.5L + 20+ min dwell → Encrypted QR token generated. "See this in store" CTA surfaced.
HANDSHAKE
Salesperson scans QR on iPad. Token resolved in 1.5s via session API. Full context transferred.
CONTEXT_LOADED
iPad shows 3D layout, cart items, fabric filters, dwell patterns. Salesperson starts from customer's endpoint, not zero.
CLOSED
Offline sale recorded. Session linked to customer account for future personalization.

The three technical decisions that made this work:

  • Encrypted, time-limited tokens: The QR code encoded a JWT token signed with a session key, valid for 4 hours. No personally identifiable data was embedded in the QR itself — just a session reference. Privacy-safe by design.
  • Anonymous sessions with opt-in identity resolution: Customers browsed anonymously. If they scanned the QR in-store, they could optionally link their account for saved preferences. We didn't require login to get the session transfer benefit.
  • The Salesperson's view was designed for closing, not browsing: The iPad UI didn't replicate the website. It surfaced the configured layout, the top 3 items by dwell time, and two pre-loaded fabric samples to have ready. It was built to arm the salesperson, not overwhelm them.

The omnichannel QR segment had both the highest close rate and highest AOV — because these were customers who had already done the research. They weren't "exploring"; they were deciding. The QR handshake removed the last friction point: the cold start in the physical environment.

The salesperson feedback was equally telling: "This is the first tool that makes me feel like I know the customer before they tell me anything." That sense of informed preparation changed the sales dynamic from reactive to consultative — the kind of experience that justifies a premium purchase.
