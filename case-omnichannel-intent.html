Context

The Setting

High-ticket consumer retail — custom furniture and full room interiors. Average order value above ₹2L. Buyer journeys don't follow clean funnels: a customer spends 45 minutes configuring a living room in a 3D editor online, saves a layout worth ₹3.8L, then stops at checkout.

This isn't abandonment. For purchases above ₹2L, 63% of customers want to see materials, touch fabrics, and talk to someone before committing. They walk into the physical store.

Problem

The Cold Start in Physical Retail

When the customer arrives in-store, the salesperson asks: "How can I help you today?"

45 minutes of intent signal — a fully configured 3D layout, fabric preferences, style filters, a considered cart — all invisible. The customer starts from zero. The digital high-intent context is completely lost in the channel transition.

The customer walked in knowing exactly what they wanted. The failure was that the salesperson had no idea the customer had already done the work.

Approaches Tried

What Didn't Work (and Why)

ApproachAdoptionWhy It Failed
Cart ID Dictation 2.3% 6-character alphanumeric code emailed to customer. Felt transactional, not premium. Salespeople found POS lookup tedious and often skipped it. Email frequently unopened before store visit.

The MVP validated the problem — customers who did use the code converted at 2x the rate. But friction in identity resolution killed adoption. The handoff needed to be as effortless as muscle memory, not a process.

Solution

The Contextual QR Handshake

A unified session state-machine that detects high-intent digital sessions and generates an encrypted, time-limited QR token. The salesperson scans it on their tablet, and the customer's full digital context loads in 1.5 seconds — zero dictation, zero login required.

QR Session Handshake — State Machine
BROWSING
Customer configures 3D room on mobile. Session graph builds in real-time.
INTENT_DETECTED
Cart > ₹1.5L + 20+ min dwell → Encrypted QR token generated. "See this in store" CTA surfaced.
HANDSHAKE
Salesperson scans QR on tablet. Token resolved in 1.5s via session API. Full context transferred.
CONTEXT_LOADED
Tablet shows 3D layout, cart items, fabric filters, dwell patterns. Salesperson starts from customer's endpoint.
CLOSED
Offline sale recorded. Session linked to customer account for future personalization.

Three design decisions that made this work:

  • Encrypted, time-limited tokens: QR encodes a JWT signed with a session key, valid for 4 hours. No personally identifiable data in the QR itself — just a session reference. Privacy-safe by design.
  • Anonymous sessions with opt-in identity: Customers browse anonymously. The QR handshake works without login. Account linking is optional — the conversion benefit doesn't require identity resolution.
  • Salesperson UI designed for closing, not browsing: The tablet view doesn't replicate the website. It surfaces the configured layout, top 3 items by dwell time, and two pre-loaded fabric samples to have ready. Built to arm the salesperson, not overwhelm them.

Key Insight

Why This Worked

Intent doesn't disappear when a customer changes channels. It just needs a bridge. The insight that friction in identity resolution kills omnichannel conversion — not lack of intent — reframed the entire problem from "how do we get online users to convert online" to "how do we make channel transitions invisible."

Salesperson feedback confirmed the shift: "This is the first tool that makes me feel like I know the customer before they tell me anything." Informed preparation changed the sales dynamic from reactive to consultative — the kind of experience that justifies a premium purchase.
