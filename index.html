01


The Story








My background is CS, but I ended up in product because the hardest problems I found weren't technical — they were the ones where you'd built the right system and people still didn't use it the way you expected.


At Delhivery, I own intracity: the algorithms that match drivers to orders, the surge pricing that adjusts in real time, and the acceptance mechanics that determine whether a driver takes a trip or waits for something better. It's a marketplace where changing one parameter affects pickup times across six cities within the hour. I've shipped things that worked, and a few that didn't.


In 2024, I started building alongside AI coding agents — Kiro, Cursor, and Claude Code. What began as prototyping driver allocation flows turned into a meaningful workflow: writing specs, generating testable code, reviewing with engineers, shipping faster. I now treat agentic coding as a PM skill, not just a developer tool. It's changed how I scope, how I communicate with eng, and how quickly I can validate assumptions.


Before Delhivery, at Urbanladder, I built UrbanInteriors from zero. The brief was vague: make interior design a business. I spent the first month manually matching customers to designers on WhatsApp before writing a single line of product spec. That manual phase is still the best product research I've done.


My MBA thesis looked at why digital payment adoption stalled in certain segments despite good UX. The answer wasn't UX — it was incentive structure and reference price framing. I use that lens constantly.




"When a metric isn't moving, the first question isn't what's broken — it's what does the user think this costs them."
